5 Climate Types

Earth has 5 major climate zones,
each with unique patterns of
temperature and rainfall

Click to explore →

🔥

Tropical

Temperature: Hot all year (25-30°C)

Rainfall: Very high

Seasons: Wet & dry (not hot & cold)

Examples: Amazon, Singapore

"Always hot, always humid!" 💧

Climate 1 of 5 • Click for next →

🏜️

Arid (Desert)

Temperature: Very hot days, cold nights

Rainfall: Less than 250mm per year

Seasons: Hot & hotter!

Examples: Sahara, Dubai

"Hot and dry, barely any rain!" ☀️

Climate 2 of 5 • Click for next →

🌳

Temperate

Temperature: Moderate (0-20°C)

Rainfall: Moderate all year

Seasons: Four distinct seasons

Examples: UK, New York

"Not too hot, not too cold!" 🍂

Climate 3 of 5 • Click for next →

❄️

Polar

Temperature: Below 10°C all year

Rainfall: Very low (but falls as snow)

Seasons: Long dark winter, short summer

Examples: Antarctica, Greenland

"Freezing cold, icy and snowy!" 🧊

Climate 4 of 5 • Click for next →

☀️

Mediterranean

Temperature: Hot, dry summers / Mild winters

Rainfall: Mostly in winter

Seasons: Hot dry summer, mild wet winter

Examples: Greece, California

"Sunny beaches and olive groves!" 🌊

Module 1: What is Climate? 🌡️

Understanding Climate

Climate is the average weather conditions in a place over a long period of time (usually 30 years or more).

Climate vs Weather

Weather = What's happening right now (today's temperature, rain, wind)

Climate = The pattern over many years (e.g., "London is usually mild and rainy")

Why Do Climates Matter?

  • 🌾 Determines what crops can grow
  • 🏠 Influences how we build our homes
  • 👕 Affects what clothes we wear
  • 🐾 Decides which animals can live there
  • 💧 Controls water availability

Module 2: Major Climate Types 🗺️

The Five Main Climate Zones

🔥 Tropical

Hot and wet all year

Temperature: 25-30°C

Rainfall: Very high (>2000mm)

Example: Amazon Rainforest, Singapore

🏜️ Arid (Desert)

Hot and very dry

Temperature: 20-40°C (but cold at night)

Rainfall: Very low (<250mm)

Example: Sahara, Arizona

🌳 Temperate

Moderate temperatures, four seasons

Temperature: -3 to 18°C

Rainfall: Moderate (500-1500mm)

Example: UK, Most of Europe

❄️ Polar

Very cold all year

Temperature: Below 10°C (often below 0°C)

Rainfall: Very low

Example: Antarctica, Arctic

☀️ Mediterranean

Hot dry summers, mild wet winters

Temperature: 10-25°C

Rainfall: Winter rain (400-900mm)

Example: Southern Spain, California

Module 3: Reading Climate Graphs 📊

What is a Climate Graph?

A climate graph (also called a climograph) shows the average temperature and rainfall for each month of the year in one place.

How to Read It:

📊
Red Line = Temperature

Shows average monthly temperature in °C (left axis)

📊
Blue Bars = Rainfall

Shows average monthly rainfall in mm (right axis)

📅
X-axis = Months

January to December (J F M A M J J A S O N D)

What to Look For:

  • Temperature range: Is it hot, cold, or moderate? Does it change much?
  • Rainfall pattern: Is it wet all year or only in certain seasons?
  • Seasons: Are there clear seasonal differences?
